Live telemetry analysis

Race Engineer listens to F1 25's UDP stream the moment you fire up a session — every channel, every car, every lap, stored locally so nothing leaves your machine.

🎙

Voice radio comms

Tap a wheel button, ask anything — "How's the rear tire?", "Pit window?", "Did Hamilton just box?". Whisper transcribes, an LLM reasons, and your engineer answers in F1-grade radio chatter.

🧠

Strategic coaching

A sandboxed team of specialist agents — tires, brakes, pace, strategy, weather, energy, driving — watches your race and only speaks up when it matters.

Under the hood

Built like a real pit wall

A Go telemetry core, a DuckDB warehouse, a React dashboard, and a team of LLM agents — all running on your machine.

01 · ingestion

Live UDP @ 20 Hz

Every F1 25 packet parsed and streamed into DuckDB. Sub-second queries across the whole session.

02 · brain

LLM strategy advisor

Bring your own key — Gemini, Claude, or OpenAI. Race-aware prompts with full session context.

03 · voice

Push-to-talk radio

Whisper STT in, edge-TTS engineer voice out. Bind it to a wheel button and forget the keyboard exists.

04 · alerts

Rule-based insights

Tire cliffs, fuel margins, damage, weather shifts, safety car — flagged the second they cross the threshold.

05 · dashboard

Real-time UI

Tire wear, brake temps, gap to leader, fuel state, lap deltas — the same dash a real engineer stares at.

06 · agents

Specialist team

Seven sandboxed personas — tires, brakes, pace, strategy, weather, energy, driving — each with its own playbook.

Setup

Three steps to your pit wall

From download to "Box, box, box" in under five minutes.

  1. STEP 01

    Enable F1 25 telemetry

    In-game: Settings → Telemetry → UDP On. Default port 20777. Nothing else to configure.

  2. STEP 02

    Launch Race Engineer

    Open the app, drop in your LLM API key, hit Start. The dashboard goes live the moment you load a session.

  3. STEP 03

    Talk to your engineer

    Hold your push-to-talk button, ask anything. The engineer answers over your headset and the agents start working the strategy.
